# Quiniela Project

FC Quiniela is a free-to-play, mobile-first web application that lets Liga MX fans pick match results every jornada (matchweek), compete inside private family and friend leagues, and track standings on a live leaderboard. The product is built specifically for the Mexican immigrant community in the United States — a deeply passionate, Liga MX-following audience that is Spanish-language dominant, highly social via WhatsApp, and underserved by existing sports-prediction apps that are built in English and modeled around sportsbook gambling.
FC Quiniela reclaims the familiar, cherished tradition of the quiniela — a friendly, zero-money prediction game played at kitchen tables and in group chats across Mexico — and brings it online with a product experience that is frictionless, culturally authentic, and shareable in seconds.

2. Problem Statement

2.1 The Opportunity
The United States is home to an estimated 36+ million people of Mexican origin. A significant portion are Liga MX fans who grew up with the quiniela as a social institution — a printed card passed around the office or family group, where everyone picks 1 (home win), X (draw), or 2 (away win) for each match of the jornada. It is a game of skill, bragging rights, and weekly ritual.

No mainstream digital product serves this ritual well. Existing fantasy and prediction apps are:
Built in English with no meaningful Spanish localization
Modeled after U.S. sports betting products, which alienates users who want a fun social game — not gambling
Not designed for the Liga MX calendar, teams, or jornada structure
Difficult to share into a WhatsApp family group in two taps

2.2 Secondary Market: Mexico
Friends and relatives still living in Mexico represent a natural secondary audience. They are already Liga MX fans and are connected to U.S.-based users via the same WhatsApp group chats. A single sharing link can pull both markets into the same private league, creating a transborder social experience around a shared passion.
